New Tax Regime vs Old Tax Regime 2025 — Which is Better For You?

Complete comparison with tax slabs, deductions, real salary examples and a free calculator to find which regime saves you more money.

📅 Updated: April 2025 ⏱️ 8 min read ✅ FY 2025-26 (AY 2026-27)

Overview — What Changed in 2025?

The Union Budget 2024 brought significant changes to the new tax regime, making it far more attractive for salaried employees. The key changes effective from FY 2025-26 (AY 2026-27) are:

  • Standard deduction under the new regime increased from ₹50,000 to ₹75,000
  • Section 87A rebate threshold raised — income up to ₹12 lakh is effectively tax-free under the new regime
  • New tax slabs introduced with a starting rate of 5% from ₹4 lakh (earlier ₹3 lakh)
  • The new regime remains the default regime — you must actively opt for old regime
💡

Key takeaway: With income up to ₹12 lakh now tax-free under the new regime, the break-even point where old regime becomes better has shifted significantly higher — to around ₹15–16 lakh CTC for most salaried employees.

Who Should Choose Which Regime?

🆕Choose New Regime if…
  • Your CTC is below ₹12 lakh (zero tax)
  • You live with family and don't pay rent
  • You have no home loan
  • You don't have significant 80C investments
  • You prefer simplicity over tax planning
  • You're a young earner early in your career
  • Your total deductions are below ₹3.75 lakh
📋Choose Old Regime if…
  • Your CTC is above ₹15 lakh
  • You pay significant rent in a metro city
  • You have an active home loan
  • You maximise 80C (PF + ELSS + LIC)
  • You pay health insurance premiums (80D)
  • You have NPS contributions under 80CCD(1B)
  • Your total deductions exceed ₹4.5 lakh

How to Switch Between Regimes

Since the new regime is now the default, you must actively opt into the old regime if you want its deductions. Here's how:

  1. Inform your employer at the start of FY — Submit a declaration (usually in April) specifying your preferred tax regime for TDS purposes.
  2. Switch at ITR filing — Salaried employees can choose a different regime at the time of filing their Income Tax Return, regardless of what was declared to the employer.
  3. Once per year rule — You can switch regimes every year. However, if you have business income, switching back from new to old regime is allowed only once in a lifetime.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is new tax regime better than old for salaried employees? +
For incomes up to ₹12 lakh, the new regime is almost always better due to the 87A rebate making it tax-free. For incomes above ₹15 lakh with significant HRA, home loan, and 80C investments, the old regime often saves more. Use the calculator above for your exact situation.
What deductions are NOT available in the new regime? +
The new regime does not allow HRA exemption, Section 80C (ELSS, PPF, LIC), Section 80D (health insurance), home loan interest under Section 24(b), LTA, 80TTA, 80E (education loan interest), and most other common deductions. Only the ₹75,000 standard deduction and employer NPS contribution are available.
Can I switch tax regime every year? +
Yes, salaried individuals with no business income can switch between old and new regimes every financial year. You need to inform your employer at the start of the year for TDS, but can make a final choice when filing your ITR.
What is the break-even point between old and new regime? +
The break-even depends on your deductions. As a rough guide: if your total eligible deductions (HRA + 80C + 80D + home loan interest) exceed ₹4–4.5 lakh, the old regime is likely better. For deductions below ₹3.5 lakh, the new regime is usually better. Use our calculator for your exact figures.
Is income up to ₹12 lakh really tax-free in 2025? +
Yes, under the new tax regime for FY 2025-26, if your taxable income (after ₹75,000 standard deduction) is up to ₹12 lakh, the rebate under Section 87A makes your effective tax liability zero. This means a salaried person with CTC up to approximately ₹13.5–14 lakh pays zero income tax under the new regime.
